I am now leading the redesign of computing education for the AI era—rebuilding a 2,000-student course around AI coding agents, creating new courses and assessment methods, and designing a new undergraduate degree. My field—computing education and the tools that support it—is practiced and recognized internationally, and my contributions to it are openly available and demonstrably adopted beyond my own institution and country.

## Computing Education for the AI Era _(primary field)_

I am redesigning computing education for a world in which AI coding agents write most of the code—across a 2,000-student introductory course, new courses for non-technical students, and a new undergraduate degree. This is novel, internationally visible work, and it has been recognized beyond my institution.

**Rebuilt CS 124 around AI coding agents.** Starting in 2026 I redesigned the larger project in [CS 124](https://cs124.org) so that **every student designs and builds their own unique Android app working with an AI coding agent (Claude Code) throughout.** When AI handles the translation from specification to code, the valuable human skill shifts from translating a specification to _formulating and directing_ one. A scaffolded pipeline—paired brainstorming, rotating pitch feedback, AI planning sessions, then an MVP followed by a menu of design, development, and evaluation challenges—moves students from "I have no idea what to build" to shipping and evaluating real software.

**Invented a way to grade open-ended projects with AI.** When every student builds something different, conventional autograding is impossible and manual grading is infeasible at scale. I built a transparent **time-on-task grading** system in which a large language model reads each student's coding-agent session logs to estimate genuine active development effort (20 hours of effort earns full credit), making creative, individualized projects gradable at 2,000-student scale.

**Kept classical programming rigorous.** Agentic development complements rather than replaces classical programming: proctored quizzes remain 70% of the grade, with **36 proctored programming challenges across 14 weekly quizzes**—far more supervised coding than most introductory courses require—so students learn both to write code themselves and to direct an agent.

**Created a course about AI, built with AI, and taught with AI.** [Using and Understanding AI](https://www.usingandunderstanding.ai/) (CS 199 UAI) is a new course for non-technical students, helping them build working mental models of how AI systems work and the judgment to use them well. Its entire platform—website, assessment system, in-class activity framework, course assistant, API server, database, and deployment infrastructure—was built **end-to-end through conversational programming with Claude Code.** Piloted in 2026, it is becoming a permanent course, **CS 108: Exploring Generative AI (2027)**.

**Built a novel two-agent conversational AI assessment.** In place of multiple-choice quizzes, students complete **chat-based oral exams conducted by AI.** A two-agent architecture separates a rubric-tracking _evaluator_ from a conversational _interviewer_; the system distinguishes understanding demonstrated independently from understanding only surfaced after prompting, and can evaluate open-ended positions, not just recall. The same system runs unchanged in a proctored computer-based testing facility, and is validated by an adversarial test harness of synthetic student personas. One assessment is open for anyone to try.

**Leading a new Applied Computing degree.** I am leading the design of a proposed four-year undergraduate degree spanning four schools at Illinois—the Siebel School of Computing & Data Science, the School of Art & Design, the Gies College of Business, and the iSchool—organized around studio-based design, domain integration, and AI-assisted building.

**Recognized for this leadership beyond my institution.** I was selected as a **Replit Faculty Fellow**—an invited fellowship from [Replit](https://replit.com/edu/faculty-fellows), a globally used AI software-development platform—and as a **Faculty AI Fellow** in the University of Illinois [Summer AI Studio](https://citl.illinois.edu/summer-ai-studio), where I provide disciplinary guidance and peer mentorship to faculty across the university integrating AI into their teaching.

## Teaching at Scale and Open Infrastructure

**Transformed CS1 at one of the world's leading computer science programs.** Since 2017 I have redesigned and led [CS 124](https://cs124.org), the introductory programming course at the University of Illinois (consistently ranked among the top five CS departments worldwide). The course now enrolls **over 2,000 students per year** (≈80% non-majors). Since I took it over, enrollment has doubled, drop rates have fallen, success rates have risen, performance in later courses has improved, and a gender performance gap has disappeared.

**Built and openly released the infrastructure behind it, with international reach.** I personally engineer the systems that make interactive computing education work at scale, and publish them as free and open resources:

- **[learncs.online](https://learncs.online)** — a freely available version of my CS1 course: 61 daily lessons in Java and Kotlin, 902 runnable code playgrounds, **2,540 interactive walkthroughs contributed by 264 instructors**, and 700+ autograded programming problems. In its first year it supported **thousands of independent learners worldwide**, who generated **hundreds of thousands of homework submissions**. It is adopted beyond Illinois—used by Ellen Spertus at Northeastern University for her Kotlin course, and as the interactive textbook for a high-school outreach program through Chicago's Discovery Partners Institute.
- **[Jeed](https://jeed.run)** — an open-source toolkit that executes untrusted Java/Kotlin code in a secure sandbox **1000× faster** than container-based alternatives, in production since 2019 and used by other educators.
- **[Questioner](https://github.com/cs124-illinois/questioner)** — a novel solution-driven authoring system that generates and validates autograders, producing accurate programming exercises an **order of magnitude faster** than prior approaches; being released for broader use at beyondgrader.com.
- **[ops-class.org](https://ops-class.org)** and **[internet-class.org](https://www.internet-class.org)** — open course frameworks (operating systems; how the internet works), including the `test161` autograder and hundreds of hours of openly available lecture video.

**Recognized leadership in the field.** Publications at **SIGCSE** (the premier international CS-education venue) in 2024 and 2025; an **invited keynote** at the Western Canadian Conference on Computing Education (2022); co-founder of the Illinois Computer Science Summer Teaching Workshop (now in its fifth year); and an active member since 2021 of an international community of practice of 100+ CS educators from dozens of institutions worldwide.
