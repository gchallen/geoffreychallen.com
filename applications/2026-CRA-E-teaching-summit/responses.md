**What are the main reasons why you want to attend this event? What do you hope to get out of attending?**

```text
AI is reshaping computer science education faster than any change I've seen in my career—rewriting what we teach, how students learn, and what it means to be a programmer. I've been building new courses and an undergraduate degree in Applied Computing designed for this moment, and rethinking my introductory courses around it. I want to attend to compare notes with other teaching-track faculty on how they're responding: where AI helps students learn versus where it short-circuits learning, how to redesign assessments and assignments when generative tools are everywhere, and what the next generation of CS curricula should look like. As someone who teaches at scale—my CS1 course reaches a couple thousand students a year—I'm especially interested in how these AI-driven changes play out across large courses. I hope to come away with concrete ideas, a stronger network of regional teaching-track colleagues, and a clearer picture of where CS education is heading.
```
